What Is Shinkansen ?

Shinkansen (Japanese: 新幹線, lit. "new main line"), often referred to as the bullet train, is a high-speed train primarily operating in Japan. Initially, the Shinkansen was built to connect distant regions of Japan with its capital, Tokyo, to aid in economic growth and development. Beyond long-distance travel, some sections around the largest metropolitan areas are used as commuter rail networks.

Shinkansen is owned by the Japan Railway Construction, Transport, and Technology Agency and operated by five companies from the Japan Railways Group.

During its more than 50 years of operation, carrying over 10 billion passengers, the Shinkansen has never experienced a single accident such as a derailment or collision, thus having no record of passenger injuries or fatalities.
